## 🚨 CRITICAL FINDINGS (REMEDIATION REQUIRED)

### 3. libWrapper Conflict: Overwritten Movement Validation
- **Problem:** The `_setupGovernanceDuel` and `_setupCounterHacks` methods both register a MIXED wrapper on `TokenDocument.prototype.update`. Because they are registered separately without a unified dispatcher, the Governance Duel wrapper effectively overwrites the Movement Validation logic for authorized tokens.
- **Impact:** Movement rules (wall collisions, stamina costs) are ignored on sovereign-controlled actors during a duel.
- **Fix Required:** Consolidate `TokenDocument.prototype.update` interception into a single unified wrapper that processes both Governance and Movement validation.

### 4. VRAM Leak: Orphaning PIXI Text Objects
- **Problem:** In `PretextOverlayManager.drawOverlay`, the `textObj` (BitmapText or Text) is removed from its parent container during the cleanup animation, but `.destroy()` is never called.
- **Impact:** Synapse accumulation in VRAM over long sessions with frequent status overlays.
- **Fix Required:** Call `textObj.destroy({ texture: false, baseTexture: false })` in the `animate` cleanup branch.

### 5. Initialization Redundancy
- **Problem:** `PretextOverlayManager.init` triggers `_initShroud` both on `canvas.ready` check AND via a `canvasReady` hook. On initial world load, this causes double initialization of the shroud container and ticker.
- **Impact:** Redundant processing and potential for duplicate containers on the interface layer.
- **Fix Required:** Use a guard flag or consolidate into a single hook entry point.
