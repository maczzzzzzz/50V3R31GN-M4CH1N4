Past the sprawl of rampant urbanism, once the streets have lost their sidewalks, lies the sprawl of Night City's burgeoning industry. Sure, Zhirafa owns the manufacturing plants in Heywood, but what about the CHOOH and steel refineries, the chemical factories, mass storage, and all the other dirty industry that keeps an isolated city of millions fed and humming?

This close to the edge of the city, the wastes have started to take over. Everything is coated in a thick layer of desert sand and dust, and the sky is perpetually tinted grey or yellow from the reflections and the pollution. The claustrophobia of the inner city's towering apartments and cloistered streets is quickly replaced by miles of piping and dull, low warehouses. The smell of city sewers, still water, low tide and garbage fade with the change, replaced by the noxious smell of unregulated chemical emissions that burns your nose on the way in and your tongue on the way out. Automated corporate security is everywhere, and the gang tagging has faded somewhat as a result.

Now all you've got to worry about is one of the autoguns malfunctioning.
