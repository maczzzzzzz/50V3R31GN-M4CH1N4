Faded brownstones line cobblestone sidewalks and well-paved streets, which are themselves clogged with corporate taxis and combat cabs, all of it crammed with the hustle and bustle of what scarcely remains of Night City's middle class. Here, you can almost forget about the Red Decades. Until you look down an alley and see the homeless camps that were pushed to the sides by the NCPD, or get up above the street noise and hear the gunshots from the combat zones, or the wind blows the wrong way from the old city center and the city PA system reminds you to take an iodine pill.

Despite the NCPD presence, it's still anything but safe. Corporate interests line the sidewalks, execs carry out deals in the shadows, and amid the crowd, if someone were to vanish, nobody would even notice.

Still, at least here you don't feel like you have to be strapped just to hit the bodega.
