Inside the double doors, a three-story-high hotel lobby greets you, lined with spacious seating arrangements of furniture on either side of the main walkway, all of which is covered by plastic sheets as though not yet ready for use. These side areas are partitioned by panels of reinforced, bulletproof wood and frosted bulletproof glass. At the front of the room, about 30 feet ahead, there are exits to other parts of the building on the left and right sides and the pathway inwards stops in front of an empty concierge's desk. Behind it are a pair of elevators, with a stair access between them.

A simulated dusk is the room’s primary source of light, shining a golden glow from above the elevators. Holographic projectors are managing an expanse of fluffy clouds beneath blue skies. Unless you frequent the Deep Badlands, this is likely the first time you’ve seen a sky this blue in 20 years - the coastal skies are either some light tone of gray or red, most of the time.
