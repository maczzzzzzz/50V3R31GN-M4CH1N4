Did you know Cyberpunk RED has an officially-published base building DLC? You can find it here.

And it just so happens, it's ripe for integration with Ticket To The Afterlife! If your players are interested in making the Afterlife their more permanent home, you can use the following integrations as a starting point.

This integration unfortunately doesn't have a scene to accompany it, currently - much as I'd like to add one, the modularity involved is outside my current scope of work. It instead operates using a container system, outlined below.

Step 1: Open The Afterlife

While you can theoretically integrate this DLC at any time, it makes the most sense to do so no earlier than after completing @UUID[JournalEntry.WqsMFILWs1Ow2ul6]{Robbin The Hood.} This gives your players the chance to get to know the Afterlife a bit, as well as it's essential staff of Vido, Sprinter, and Sammy.

Step 2: Floor 4, For The In-House Problem Solvers

Once the Afterlife is open, its management would like to show its appreciation to the group of edgerunners who made it possible, by giving them the opportunity to rent out Floor 4 at a steeply discounted rate… with strings attached. This opportunity is only available as follows:

At least half the group (rounded up) needs to live on the floor.

They have to come when called - the party must complete at least one job for the Afterlife a month.

Once your table completes the entirety of Ticket To The Afterlife, this restriction can be removed.

The party has to make rent each month, regardless of who's living on the floor. It's a lump sum, so cough up!

The party must retain a clean, presentable appearance that properly reflects the Afterlife's status in Night City while they stay on Floor 4.

Certain features are only available for purchase when certain services are unlocked. Those are detailed below.

Step 3: Initial Purchase

Floor 4 is highly modular, made up of four quarters of two rooms each for a max of 8 rooms. The cost of the floor varies, depending on how much of it the party wants to occupy, as follows:

The first room which the party decides to rent costs 1700eb/month.

Instead of paying the eb required to rent a normal studio, this cost can be met by paying 8 Eagles.

After rent for the first room has been purchased, the second room costs 1500eb/7 Eagles.

All subsequent rooms cost 1250eb/6 Eagles.

For all expenses an Eagle counts as 200eb towards making rent - any odd math there is a discount provided for paying the full cost in Eagles rather than cash.

The first time a party member purchases a room, the party gains access to the @UUID[Actor.9gsJzZawFxaMaJa3]{Afterlife Floor 4 Rooms & Upgrades} container.

Right click the actor -> Configure Ownership -> All Players -> Owner

This container both acts as the party's stash, and tracks the rooms on the floor and their upgrades in one easy to access central location.

For each room which the party is renting, add 1 @UUID[Item.ioAgmJa3gArdDAhF]{Afterlife Apartment gear item} to the stash, and rename it with either the room's owner or its number.

Each room comes with 4 Option Slots - these are filled via purchase of the HQ Upgrade items which are also included with the module.

Some upgrades do not require option slots, as they're part of the hotel - check descriptions below, and/or item descriptions.

Each room comes with two base upgrades - the @UUID[Item.IvJzbqAIa3ij818a]{Night City Queen} and a @UUID[Item.HotAFKkR0HzfLJFo]{SmartHouse Kitchenette} - which can each be swapped out with a different, purchased upgrade by spending 1 downtime day each to do so.

Each room also comes with a @UUID[Item.IafVRGPmEInNWkIN]{Golden Throne Bath System,} which cannot be altered in any way.

The Owner insists that anyone staying in the hotel has access to a shower, which they use regularly, lest they face eviction.

Instead of using the Eurobuck roster on the Floor 4 container to track cash, use it to track earned and spent Headquarters IP (outlined next).

Step 4: Earn HQ IP

Every GM awards IP differently, but for Floor 4, I recommend implementing the following group progression… at least for Headquarters IP specifically.

For every sitting that the party plays through where IP isn't awarded, the party earns 20 HQ IP.

Some missions will take multiple sittings to complete, despite still being a single "session" of content - this rule accounts for that time.

For every mission which is completed, award Headquarters IP according to their success as you judge it via the Group column of the Improvement Point table on pg 410 of the core rules, in addition to any IP you provide your players.

Step 5: Upgrade Your HQ

Any player who rents a room at the Afterlife can spend HQ IP at any time they're idle. Removing an existing room upgrade requires 1 downtime day; installing an upgrade requires 4 hours, but is done by the Afterlife staff and the character who ordered the upgrade need not be present for it.

Important note: some upgrades have lightly modified functionality compared to those in the DLC - see item entries of each upgrade to confirm functionality before installing.

The list of upgrades are as follows - click for descriptions or see associated items folder.

A bed that sleeps 2 (or 1 big boy) and gives a temporary +1 bonus to the first check made after sleeping on it.

Allows 3 edgerunners to prep food, and provides a 10% refund on lifestyle costs.

Allows 3 edgerunners to keep clean, providing temporary bonuses to Personal Grooming checks.

Provides bonuses to the business conducted by Fixers while it's done in the lounge, and if upgraded, makes it easier for Fixers and Medias to take meetings.

Locked To Missions
