If you don't have enemies, you're just not playing the game right. And if you meet someone who says THEY don't have enemies… you know they're lying. Or they're boring.

Finding, confronting, and defeating enemies is a fun and engaging way to involve your character more in a scene. Throughout the campaign you'll have the opportunity to make/defeat quite a few.

There's no strict cap on how many enemies a person can make, but you probably won't want to juggle more than 4 at a time. Unless your GM says otherwise, I recommend rolling the enemy path found in the core rules on p. 51/52, and always starting the game with at least 1.

If you ever end up with no enemies, roll a new one at the end of a mission. How they're introduced is at GM discretion, but I recommend making them survivors of your antics, or people who you've otherwise pissed off while adventuring.

Enemies have the opportunity to pop up on just about any mission, as the leaders of the opposition - in some cases they might not even be aware that they're against you - in others, they'll be showing up just because they know you're involved.

Once you've personally defeated one of your enemies, you gain 1 personal rep, which expires in 1 in-game month.

Note that "defeated" doesn't mean killed. Sometimes it's as simple as a bullet in the head - others, you'll be better suited to costing them status, or even choosing to forgive and befriend them - kill them with kindness!
