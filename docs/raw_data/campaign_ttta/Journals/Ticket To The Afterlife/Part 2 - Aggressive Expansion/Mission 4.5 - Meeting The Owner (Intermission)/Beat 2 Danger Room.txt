Rules of Engagement

Beyond the door on the other end of the hall is a sight that would catch even the most hardened veteran off guard.

Somehow, 10 meters below the ground, a techno-god has cut out a slice of South Night City, and plopped it down right here in front of you. Some of it feels more real than other parts - the walls are glowing with holograms, and behind them you can make out technology. The same is true for the props in the room - rather than real props, the objects are hexes and squares, projected onto smart surfaces.

But running a hand along the ground feels like dirt and cracked concrete, even if your hand comes up clean. A decrepit sofa near the doors feels like it's made of worn-down, weather-broken cloth, and yields properly when you put force on it. And a hot, stiff wind blows up, wafting the smell of the overflowing garbage bins into your nose.

It's not actually South Night City - but as far as replicas go, it's pretty damn close.

Behind you, the door shuts and amidst your gawking, you notice for the first time that Vido and the owner haven't followed you in. That isn't left a mystery for long, though, as a phone line connects and the Owner's voice echoes over the scene.

"Welcome to the Afterlife's Danger Room," she says. You can't see her, obviously, but she sounds impressed. "Normally it's pay to play, but today you get to go for free. Vido, the rules of engagement."

Now would be a good time to go over the rules for the Danger Room, and make them visible to your players if you haven't already. They can be found in the Services Of The Afterlife journals, entry 3.

In addition to the rules contained there, Rogue and/or Vido outline the following:

Today, the players will be pitted against a number of scav simulations, to test the simulation against edgerunners who are experienced in dealing with them, and to test the edgerunners for the Owner's satisfaction.

The group will be pitted against waves (outlined in the setup section for this beat), until all are beaten or the party concedes.

As a courtesy, the pair of fixers reveal the stat block of the scav savage via the floor's secondary function, though the full functionality and ability to spend for the other statblocks won't be unlocked until after this encounter.

They do so to make sure the party is aware that critical injuries will still actually injure you, even if the rest of the damage is more-or-less nonlethal.

Once the party signals that they're ready to begin, combat commences.

Players may not ready actions prior to combat beginning, though they are allowed to move anywhere in the scene before signalling their readiness.

They're also free to spend a few minutes discussing strategy.

Once the simulation begins, the scavs can spawn anywhere, at GM discretion - including, for example, right behind a character in cover.

Concluding the Combat

"Alright - that's enough."

The Owner's voice rings through the simulation and instantly, everything freezes. Corpses melt away into nothingness. Even the simulated environment itself fades, revealing a nondescript blue room built entirely from hex platforms and tiles made from durable-looking smart materials.

"I've seen enough, and I've seen worse. You all are approved for the next set of jobs. Don't let me down, folks. Oh, and one more thing…"

At this point, tally up the results of Rogue's assessment, by character - each of the passed checks from beat 1's roleplaying encounter count as 1 point, and each of the combat contest criteria for the Danger Room count as 1 point (who did the most damage, etc.)

Rogue was particularly impressed with anyone who scores 3 or more points; anyone who does so immediately gains 1 personal rep, as she promises to promote them in particular to anyone who asks.

Once that's communicated, the intermission ends; the party is sent on their way, though Vido notes that Sammy will have more work for them soon.
