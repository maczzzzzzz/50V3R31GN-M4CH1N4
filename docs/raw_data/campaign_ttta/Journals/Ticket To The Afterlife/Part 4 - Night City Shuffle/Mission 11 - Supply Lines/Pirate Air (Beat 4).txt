Gyro Attackers

The gyros appear in the sky around the zepplin, each and all of them 100 meters off the edge of the map in their respective direction (that's 2.5 turns of movement, since 1 move = 2 m/yds and gyros have a move of 20) and immediately roll initiative. The raiders aboard are screaming and hollering as they approach the craft, slinging grapple hooks and firing weapons into the air.

When they get close to the zep, the craft do the following:

1/3rd of the craft (or whereabouts) head for the crew deck, and cover one another while their raiders are dropped onto the landing platform.

1/3rd of the craft (or whereabouts) head to the top of the craft, dropping their crew onto the roof, where they'll head for the ladder down into the cargo bay.

1/3rd of the craft circle the cargo deck - as they approach the gun hatches, their crew will, one at a time, sling grappling hooks to catch the guns and swing over before climbing up.

This requires the scav make a DV15 Physical check; on failure they're unsuccessful.

Only 1 scav from each craft can attempt this each gyro turn.

On success, the scav is immediately transported just outside the main gun, 1d10 (minimum 5) meters below the gun.

Empty gyros, or those waiting to drop their crew, provide suppressive fire or attempt to disable the guns on the zep's main deck.

Do note, though, that the scavs are just as interested in the zep itself as they are in its cargo, and will try not to damage it beyond operation.

Scavs don't have radios, but the zep has an external intercom; communication can be attempted via the bridge.

Once aboard, the scavs will attempt to seize control of the ship; if they get to the bridge, they immediately take the zep off course to their encampment, taking 20 minutes to arrive.

More reinforcements likely await here, at GM discretion.
