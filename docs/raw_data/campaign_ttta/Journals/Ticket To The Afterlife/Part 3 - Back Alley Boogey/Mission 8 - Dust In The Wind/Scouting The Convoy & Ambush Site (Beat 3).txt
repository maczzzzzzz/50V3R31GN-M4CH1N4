Scouting The Convoy

Thanks to Ranger's expertise, scouting the convoy without being seen isn't too difficult. However, a sandstorm is kicking up - while not yet that intense (and a good way to make sure the convoy can't call for help), it's impairing visibility. Have the party member(s) scouting the convoy make a perception DV15 perception check. On a fail, they know the convoy is there but can't learn much else. Reveal the following as the result improves:

Result

Information

16+

The party learns the number of vehicles, and their type, but not any specific details.

18+

The party also learns how the vehicles are outfitted - what guns are present, etc.

20+

The party learns the number of crew assigned to the convoy, and a rough estimate of their power level (yes strike commander, no brute or demo, etc.)

22+

The party gleans everything they could want to know about the convoy, including the armaments of those manning it.

Scouting The Ambush Site

Ranger is much more excited about the prospect of a moving ambush than just stopping the convoy, but provides an option for doing so all the same. Racing ahead of the lumbering hauler and its escorts, he shows the party scout(s) a decrepit highway overpass. His suggestion to stop the convoy is to plant dynamite beneath the overpass, demolishing it as the convoy approaches and throwing the Miilitech forces into disarray.

Take this time to show the scout(s) the @Scene[Rr3BAJcbh6mzathM]{Badlands Ambush} scene, and give them the opportunity to assess their plan of attack.

Ranger really wants to do the "moving heist" option though, because it's way cooler, and will say as much.

Does the Sandstorm Intensify?

In either ambush scenario, a moderate sandstorm stops Militech from calling for help. The storm isn't severe enough to impact the operation, however - assuming the players are wearing dust-protective gear provided by the nomads, they shouldn't face any difficulties.

As an optional extra challenge, however, the sandstorm can be intensified, to the following effects:

Impeded visibility: Any perception checks or shots made beyond 10m are made at a cumulative -2 penalty for each 10m beyond the first.

Keep it on the road: Any kind of vehicle maneuvers are made at a -4 penalty.

It's coarse and rough, and gets everywhere: When a weapon is fired, it jams on a 1 and requires an action to clear.

Once the party has decided on their plan of attack, and the GM has decided if the sandstorm intensifies or not, proceed to Beat 4a or 4b.
