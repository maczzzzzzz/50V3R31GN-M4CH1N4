Is your party struggling to come up with a good way to approach the office without jeopardizing the mission?

Some parties relish the challenge of an unexpected obstacle. Others, though…

I've had it both ways - in one playtest, my party spent 20 minutes brainstorming ideas for how to get into the office and save Silvers' kid without putting anyone in harm's way. And in another, the problem seemed insurmountable until I provided them with some in-character knowledge as to how they might approach to the otherwise.

If you as the GM have some ideas and your players are stumped, try having them make tactics or deduction checks for general suggestions, or use more particular skills (business, streetwise, local expert, etc.) based on character backgrounds.

Regardless of how you deliver it, though, here's some role suggestions for different plan ideas.

Fixers could try and source the information on where Bobby is being held without ever interacting with the exec at all.

Execs from the same company could try and intimidate Silvers' boss into giving up the information, or could represent a rival company looking to buy out Silvers' contract.

Netrunners could hack the computer system to find where Bobby is being held, if they're given the opportunity.

Solos could create a distraction, drawing the exec out of the office to make life easier.

Medias could sus out blackmail or rumors which lead to leverage over the exec.

Lawmen could threaten the exec in a legal capacity, assuming they have the power and authority to do so.

And so on.
